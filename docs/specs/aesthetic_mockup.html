k-Terminus

Design System · Zen Brutalism · 間 (Ma) — the space between

Philosophy

Japanese brutalism treats raw material not as confrontation but as vessel. Tadao Ando's exposed concrete exists so that a thin slit of light has something to cut through. The brutality is in service of the serene.

This design system applies that principle to infrastructure tooling. Borders are formwork lines — visible, honest, but quiet. The mauve accent appears sparingly, like light through a precise cut in concrete. Interactive objects have weight and presence like stones placed in a dry garden. Everything else is 間 — meaningful emptiness.

Structural chrome is minimal: 1px borders, no shadows, no decoration. It recedes. Interactive elements — machine cards, buttons, topology nodes — have weight: 2px borders, subtle shadows, the full mauve accent. The terminal content is the innermost void — the quietest, most spacious layer where the actual work happens.

Design Notes

Intensity hierarchy: Structural chrome (sidebar edge, header, tab bars) uses 1px borders and no shadows — it recedes like the walls of a room. Interactive containers (machine cards, topology nodes) carry 2px borders and subtle presence. Action elements (buttons) get the full treatment. The terminal void is the innermost sanctum — maximum breathing room, minimum distraction.

The light slit: The mauve accent never floods a surface. It appears as a 2px vertical line on selected cards, a 1px underline on active tabs, a subtle glow on focus states. Like light entering through a precise cut in Ando's concrete — it's powerful because it's constrained.

Breathing animation: Status indicators don't pulse aggressively — they breathe, with a slow 3-second ease-in-out cycle. The cursor blinks with an ease curve, not a step function. Everything organic, nothing mechanical.

Concrete texture: A fractal noise overlay at 3% opacity sits over everything, giving surfaces the subtle grain of poured concrete. It prevents the digital-flatness that makes dark UIs feel sterile.
